# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students of ICFAI (founders and aspiring founders), mentors (faculty and alumni reviewers), and admins (program staff). Students browse the feed between classes on laptops and phones, submit ideas to the gated pipeline, post and solve real-world problems, and recruit teammates. Mentors work in review queues: scoring idea submissions and problem solutions. Admins moderate content, manage members, and run the calendar. The job to be done: take a campus startup idea from first post to a vetted, team-backed venture inside one trusted network.

## Product Purpose

IFN (ICFAI Founders Network) is the digital home of the university's founder community: a members-only platform combining a discussion feed, a stage-gated idea pipeline (G1-G6 with mentor review), a problem hub with community solutions, team acquisition, a directory, and an events calendar. It exists so that student ventures get structured, credible progression instead of dying in group chats. Success: ideas reach later gates, problems collect scored solutions, and teams form on-platform.

## Brand Personality

Institutional, credible, focused. The interface should feel like it is issued by the university's entrepreneurship cell: composed, quietly confident, built to be trusted by faculty and students alike. Young energy comes from the content and typography, not from loud color or playful chrome. Emotional goal: "my work is taken seriously here."

## Anti-references

- Generic AI SaaS: Inter everywhere, purple-blue gradients, glowing pill buttons, identical icon-heading-text card grids, hero metrics.
- Twitter/X clone: borrowed social-app shell language, Twitter's exact grays and greens, engagement-bait visual grammar.

## Design Principles

1. The work is the hero: posts, ideas, and solutions carry the page; chrome stays quiet.
2. Issued, not branded: visual authority comes from restraint and consistency, like university print material, not from decoration.
3. Every role sees its job: student, mentor, and admin each land on screens where the next action is obvious.
4. One system everywhere: the same tokens, type scale, and components on every page; no one-off styling.
5. Earn trust at the edges: empty states, errors, and moderation actions are written and designed with the same care as the happy path.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: body text contrast >= 4.5:1 (including placeholder and muted text), all flows keyboard-operable, visible focus states, labeled controls, prefers-reduced-motion honored on every animation. No information carried by color alone (role badges and status chips keep text labels).
