# Welcome to the ICFAI Founders Network

A private home for ICFAI founders and the people who guide them. This guide covers everything you can do across the network — from the community spaces to the tools for reviewing and advancing ideas.

---

## 1. Getting started

### Your account
- If your account was set up for you, you'll receive an email with a temporary password. Sign in with it, then change it in **Settings**.
- If you registered yourself, open the confirmation link we email you, then sign in.
- Forgot your password? On the login screen choose **Forgot password** and follow the email.

### Setting up your profile
The first time you sign in, you'll complete a short profile: your name, region, sector, and domain, plus optional details. This takes a minute and unlocks the rest of the app. Edit it anytime from **Profile** or **Settings**.

---

## 2. Finding your way around

- **Left rail** — Feed, Idea Pipeline, Problem Hub, Team Acquisition, Directory, the Autopsy Library, and your review workspace.
- **Top bar** — a calendar shortcut, the **bell** for notifications, and your **profile menu**.
- **The bell** — recent notifications and upcoming events. It includes filters to focus on what needs you (see §6).

---

## 3. The community spaces

- **Feed** — share updates, run polls, raise problems, vote, and comment. Pinned posts sit on top.
- **Problem Hub** — real, unsolved problems with open solution threads. A great place to point founders.
- **Team Acquisition** — *Hiring for your startup? Post a role. Want to join one? Apply.* Post role needs or apply to others'; posters review applicants.
- **Directory** — search every member by role, sector, or region.
- **Autopsy Library** — honest post-mortems of failed ideas and the lessons behind them.
- **Calendar** — upcoming sessions, deadlines, and events.

## 4. The Idea Pipeline

A guided path that takes an idea from first submission to incubation. Every idea carries its own **IFN-#** reference and moves through six gates.

| Gate | Stage | What it means |
|------|-------|---------------|
| **G1** | Idea Submitted | The application is in the queue, waiting to be picked up. |
| **G2** | Assigned | An administrator has assigned the idea, awaiting acceptance. |
| **G3** | Picked Up | The founder completes the full dossier — details and files. |
| **G4** | Review Completed | The dossier passed the rubric; the founder submits a beta plan. |
| **G5** | Beta Prototyping | Building. Advancing requires evidence — a prototype link or files. |
| **G6** | Incubation | Confirmed for incubation; work continues via actions and the thread. |

Each idea shows a status chip — **Your move**, **With reviewer**, **In the queue**, **Needs admin**, or **Done** — so it's always clear who holds it next. An idea can stay **Active**, be sent back to **Refine & retry**, or be **Rejected**.

## 5. Reviewing and advancing ideas

Open your review workspace from **Mentor Review** in the left rail. This is where assigned ideas live and where you move them forward.

### The workflow
1. **Accept an assignment.** When an idea is assigned to you, it appears in your workspace. Accept it to pick it up (this moves the idea to *Picked Up*).
2. **Read the dossier.** Review the founder's details, files, and thread.
3. **Score on the rubric.** Evaluate against the seven criteria:
   - Clarity
   - Feasibility
   - Market potential
   - Innovation
   - Technical strength
   - Scalability
   - Problem–solution fit
4. **Decide.** Advance the idea to the next gate, or send it back as **Refine & retry** with guidance. Refine is the most useful tool you have — be specific about what would make it stronger.
5. **Keep the thread alive.** Use the idea's thread and actions to keep momentum between gates, especially through **Beta Prototyping**.

### Evidence and funding
To advance an idea out of **Beta Prototyping**, look for real evidence — a working prototype link or supporting files. When the prototype needs funding to progress, there's a path to flag it for incubation funding.

> **Tip:** A clear, specific *Refine & retry* note saves rounds of back-and-forth. Tie every request to a rubric criterion.

## 6. Notifications

The bell and the full notifications page give you focused views:

- **Needs action** — what's waiting on you right now.
- **All activity** — the broader stream across your work.
- **Mine** — items you're involved in.

Choose **Expand notifications** to open the full page. The badge on the bell shows unread items.

---

## 7. Settings and your account

From **Settings** you can update your profile, change your password, set notification preferences, and sign out of every device at once.

---

## 8. If something looks off

- **Read-only or suspended account:** an administrator has restricted it — contact the IFN team if that's unexpected.
- **Can't post:** posting may be temporarily paused.
- **Trouble signing in:** use **Forgot password**; after several quick attempts a short cooldown applies.
